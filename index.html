ABOUT ME.

My name is Michal Ruprecht. I am a Detroit-based health and science journalist currently finishing my last year at the University of Michigan.

I am the son of immigrants who fled an oppressive political system, where the freedom of the press was nonexistent. As a bilingual first-generation American, I attended ESL classes and grew up in the borough of Queens, N.Y., a community that oftentimes avoided the doctor’s office because of historical injustices.

I began reporting for my high school newspaper to improve my writing. But the experience morphed into something more than a writing class. Journalism has become a platform for me to write the kind of disruptive stories that contribute to improved scientific and medical literacy among medically-underserved communities.

Since 2019, I have worked on three impactful investigations about alleged medical malpractice, abuse in research and COVID-19 violations in a restaurant as a beat reporter and assistant editor at The Michigan Daily, my student newspaper. One investigation was published and my article led to an improved culture and anonymous reporting system in an academic department. I also led coverage of a ​​conjoined twin separation (the first of its kind in the state of Michigan), the development of a universal flu vaccine and the adoption of telehealth during the pandemic.

I'm currently a remote intern for The Nation's Health, a publication of the American Public Health Association.

Outside of work, I am passionate about brewing kombucha, ceramics, NPR podcasts, Costco, yoga, swimming, running and traveling. Find some of my favorite clips below. I can be reached at me@michalruprecht.com or on Twitter.
